Chile's annual inflation rate slowed to 4.4% in May 2025 from 4.5% in April, marking the 17th consecutive month that inflation remained above the Chilean Central Bank's 2% - 4% target. The decline was driven by price deceleration in 9 of the 13 subsectors. Inflation fell for recreation and culture (1.9% vs. 2.2%), home maintenance (1.7% vs. 2.7%) and restaurants and hotels (6.4% versus 6.7%), while transportation prices swung to deflation (-0.1% vs. 0.4%). On the other hand, prices rose in food and non-alcoholic beverages (4.6% versus 4.4% in April) and housing and public services (10.9% versus 10.6%). In the monthly comparison, consumer prices increased by 0.2% in May, remaining stable compared to the 0.2% recorded in the previous month and in line with market projections.
source:

Chile Inflation Rate Slows to 4.5% in April
Chile’s annual inflation rate slowed to 4.5% in April 2025 from 4.9% in March, marking the 16th consecutive month that inflation remained above the Chilean central bank’s 2% - 4% target. The decline was driven by a deceleration of prices in 9 of the 13 subsectors. Inflation fell for food and non-alcoholic beverages (4.4% vs. 5.1% in March), recreation and culture, (2.2% vs. 2.4%), housing and utilities (10.6% vs. 11%), restaurants and hotels (6.7% vs. 6.8%), and transportation (0.4% vs. 1.6%). On a monthly basis, consumer prices increased by 0.2% in March, slowing from the 0.5% increase recorded in the previous month and in line with market forecasts.

Chile Inflation Rate Rises to 4.9% in March
Chile's annual inflation rate accelerated to 4.9% in March 2025, up from 4.7% in February, matching January's 15-month high. It also marked the 11th consecutive month that inflation has remained above the central bank's target range of 2% to 4%, primarily driven by a sharp rise in food and non-alcoholic beverages (5.1% vs. 2.9% in February). Prices for recreation and culture also increased at a faster pace (2.4% vs. 2.3%), while price growth slowed in housing and utilities (11% vs. 11.6%), restaurants and hotels (6.8% vs. 7.2%), and transportation (1.6% vs. 2.7%). On a monthly basis, consumer prices rose by 0.5% in March, accelerating from the 0.4% increase recorded in the previous month and matching market forecasts. Core consumer prices rose 0.4%, following a 0.3% increase in the previous month.
