Producer Prices in Brazil decreased to 171.17 points in April from 171.79 points in March of 2025, the lowest since November 2024. A fall of 0.36% month-over-month in April 2025, easing from a 0.62% drop in March and marking the third straight month of deflation. Prices declined in both the extractive (-4.43%) and transformation (-0.17%) sectors. Annually, producer prices rose 7.27%, the slowest pace in five months, down from 8.37% in March.
source:
Getulio Vargas Foundation
Producer Prices in Brazil averaged 97.29 points from 1950 until 2025, reaching an all time high of 576.38 points in November of 2013 and a record low of 0.00 points in February of 1950.
