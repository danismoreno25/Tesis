function SetTheme(isDark) {
        console.log('Changing theme', isDark);
        if (IsDarkMode != isDark) {
            IsDarkMode = isDark;
            document.body.classList.toggle("dark-theme");
            // Set cookie
            const d = new Date();
            d.setTime(d.getTime() + (365 * 24 * 60 * 60 * 1000));
            let expires = "expires=" + d.toUTCString();
            document.cookie = "IsDarkMode" + "=" + IsDarkMode + ";" + expires + ";path=/";
            IsDarkMode ? ga('event', 'te_darkmode_on') : ga('event', 'te_darkmode_off');
            window.location.reload();
        }
    }

Ecuador Inflation Rate
In Ecuador, the main components of the consumer price index are: Food and Non-Alcoholic Beverages (25 percent of the total weight); Transport (14 percent); Housing, Water, Electricity, Gas and Other Fuels (10 percent) and Clothing and Footwear (9 percent). Restaurants and Hotels account for 7 percent; Education for 7 percent; Furniture and Household Equipment for 6 percent and Health Care for 6 percent. Others include: Leisure and Culture (5.9 percent); Other Goods and Services (5.2 percent); Communications (4.4 percent) and Alcoholic Beverages, Tobacco and Narcotics (1 percent). The national index has a base of 100 as of 2004. The CPI reflects the prices and spending patterns registered in eight representative cities: Quito, Guayaquil, Cuenca, Machala, Esmeraldas, Manta, Ambato and Loja, which account for 67.44 percent of the urban population of the country.

Ecuador Inflation Rate Hits 7-Month High

Public services registered the most significant annual increase and rebounded after being in deflationary territory (13.54% vs. 3.33% in May), as well as food and non-alcoholic beverages (0.78% vs. 0.39%), and hotels and restaurants (1.44% vs. 1.26%). Ecuador Inflation Rate Rebounds from a 4-Year Low
Consumer prices in Ecuador rose by 0.46% year-on-year in April 2025, rebounding from a 0.69% decrease in the previous month, which marked the first annual decline in consumer prices since June 2021. Utilities recorded the most significant year-on-year increase and rebounded after being in deflationary territory (3.33% vs. -9.64% in May), as well as food and non-alcoholic beverages (0.39% vs. -0.20%), while prices accelerated for hotel and restaurants (1.26% vs. 0.98%). On a monthly basis, consumer prices rose by 1.03%, accelerating from a 0.27% increase in the previous month.

Ecuador Inflation Rate Nears 4-Year Low
Consumer prices in Ecuador fell by 0.69% year-on-year in April 2025, following a 0.31% increase in the previous month. This marked the first annual decline in consumer prices since June 2021, driven by broad-based price drops across all categories. Utilities recorded the most significant year-on-year decrease, though the rate of deflation eased compared to the previous month (-9.64% vs -14.94%). Prices also declined for clothing and footwear (-2.48% vs -1.04%), the communication sector (-0.98% vs +1.24%), and furniture, household items, and home maintenance. On a monthly basis, consumer prices rose by 0.27%.
